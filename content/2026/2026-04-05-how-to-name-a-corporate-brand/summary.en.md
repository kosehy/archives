# Summary — How to Choose a Corporate Brand Name

## Core idea

This Korean blog post explains that choosing a corporate brand name is not just a creative task. It requires coordinated checking across the corporate registry, trademark search, and domain availability.

## Key points

- Prepare several candidate names that match the business purpose.
- Check for conflicts in the corporate registry.
- Use KIPRIS to review trademark availability in the relevant category.
- Check and reserve matching English-form domains.
- Prefer names that are easy to say, familiar, memorable, and image-evoking.
- Compound words or newly invented words are often more practical than simple generic words.
- Trademark agents and legal professionals can process filings, but the naming decision itself belongs to the founder.

## Bottom line

The article’s main message is that a good corporate brand name survives three filters at once: registry availability, trademark viability, and domain availability.
