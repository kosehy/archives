# Summary — From prompts to harnesses: four years of AI agentic patterns

## Core idea

This article argues that the center of engineering rigor in AI systems has shifted three times in four years: from prompt engineering, to context engineering, to harness engineering.

## Key points

- The article frames 2022–2026 as three successive eras: prompts, context, and harnesses.
- Each shift happened because the previous paradigm could not reliably deliver what it promised.
- Prompt engineering failed because wording alone could not overcome missing context, non-determinism, and production complexity.
- Context engineering improved results by focusing on what information enters the model, especially through retrieval, compression, isolation, and cache-aware design.
- Even context engineering proved insufficient, because long-running agents also require error recovery, security boundaries, evaluation, and execution control.
- Harness engineering is presented as the next layer: the full surrounding system of rules, tools, evaluators, feedback loops, and operational constraints.
- The article insists that rigor did not disappear with AI coding tools; it relocated upward into system design.

## Bottom line

The main thesis is that modern AI engineering is no longer mainly about crafting better prompts. It is about designing the systems that shape, constrain, and verify agent behavior.
