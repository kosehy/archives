# Summary

This article discusses a case involving an international student who was caught doing illegal one-day construction work and then received cancellation of status and a departure order. The core point is that illegal employment is not a minor administrative issue. It can affect future visa issuance or status changes, especially for D-10 and E-7 pathways.

The article explains that immigration review tends to consider the duration of the violation, the size of the penalty, whether the conduct was repeated, and whether there was intent. In this particular case, the violation lasted only one day, the penalty was relatively low, there was no prior violation history, and the student promptly admitted the issue and complied.

A court ultimately cancelled the departure order by applying the principle of proportionality. It found that the public benefit of the order did not outweigh the serious disadvantage to the student, especially given strong ties to Korean society. Still, the article stresses that this should not be treated as a general rule, since illegal employment usually causes serious immigration problems.
