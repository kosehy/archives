# Summary — The Lighthouse Playbook

## Core idea

This article argues that startups should build signal by helping already-aligned people become more visible and trusted, rather than relying on direct brand amplification or transactional influencer marketing.

## Key points

- Early startups need signal to attract hires, customers, and partners.
- In abundance environments, signal comes more from what you give away than from what you claim.
- Growth is framed as a problem of preferential attachment and trust transfer.
- “Lighthouses” are aligned people who can authentically transmit your worldview to the networks you want to reach.
- Good lighthouses may be employees, customers, advisors, or founders.
- The practical move is to help these people build their own brands and visibility.
- A compelling narrative or mission is necessary so the signal feels meaningful rather than promotional.
- Networks of lighthouses compound more strongly than isolated examples.

## Bottom line

The article’s main claim is that startup trust scales best when you invest in other people’s signal, and their credibility brings others toward you.
