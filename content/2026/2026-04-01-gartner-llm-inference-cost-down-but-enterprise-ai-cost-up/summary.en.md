## One-paragraph summary
This article argues that falling LLM inference costs will not necessarily make enterprise AI cheaper overall. Gartner expects steep declines in token-level inference costs by 2030 due to better chips, improved infrastructure, model design advances, and broader edge deployment. But as enterprises shift toward agentic and reasoning-heavy AI, token consumption per task rises so sharply that total spending may still increase. The article’s strategic conclusion is that future value will concentrate not in a single model, but in platforms that can orchestrate workloads across multiple model types efficiently.

## Key points
- Gartner forecasts a more than 90% decline in inference costs for trillion-parameter LLMs by 2030.
- Cost efficiency could improve by as much as 100 times relative to similarly sized models in 2022.
- Lower token prices do not automatically mean lower enterprise AI spending.
- AI agents use far more tokens per task than traditional chatbots.
- Advanced reasoning systems still depend on scarce compute and system capacity.
- Cheap tokens can temporarily hide architectural inefficiency.
- Multi-model orchestration is presented as a core future capability.
- Smaller or domain-specific models should handle routine work, while frontier models should be reserved for high-value reasoning.

## Memorable takeaway
AI may become cheaper per token while becoming more expensive per business system.
