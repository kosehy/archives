# Gartner: LLM inference costs will fall 90% by 2030, but enterprise AI spending may still rise

Original: https://www.hellot.net/mobile/article.html?no=111774
Saved: 2026-04-01
Translation note: Smoothed into natural English for readability while preserving the article’s claims.

Gartner forecasts that by 2030, the inference cost of large language models with one trillion parameters will fall by more than 90% compared with 2025. Improvements in semiconductor and infrastructure efficiency, model design innovation, wider deployment of inference-optimized chips, and broader use of edge devices are cited as key drivers of cost reduction. Gartner expects that by 2030, LLMs could become as much as 100 times more cost-efficient than similarly sized models in the early phase of 2022.

However, the article argues that falling token prices will not automatically reduce enterprise AI spending. More advanced AI capabilities are structurally tied to much higher token usage. AI agents require between 5 and 30 times more tokens per task than conventional chatbots, while also covering a much broader range of work. As a result, even if token prices decline, total inference costs may still rise because usage is growing even faster.

A Gartner senior director analyst warned that basic AI functionality is approaching near-zero cost, but the computing resources and systems needed to support advanced reasoning remain scarce. Companies that mask inefficient architectures behind currently cheap token prices may hit limits when they try to scale agent-based AI.

Gartner therefore expects value to concentrate in platforms that can orchestrate workloads efficiently across a diverse model portfolio. Repetitive and high-frequency tasks should be handled by smaller models or domain-specific language models, while expensive frontier models should be used selectively for high-value, complex reasoning tasks. The article argues that moving away from dependence on a single model toward a multi-model orchestration strategy will become a decisive source of AI competitiveness.
