## Overview

This article explains the distinction in Korea between:

- a company’s registered legal name
- a trade name used in business

It argues that confusing the two can create practical and legal risk, especially in registration, contracting, invoicing, branding, and dispute situations.

## Why the two are often confused

The article says the terms sound similar and are often used loosely, but they carry different legal meanings.

- The corporate legal name is the official registered name of the company.
- The trade name is the outward-facing business name used on signs, business cards, and marketing materials.

A company may legally be registered as one thing and market itself under another.

## Corporate name

The corporate name is the formal name entered in the corporate registry when the company is incorporated. It is the legal subject used in contracts, litigation, and official records.

## Trade name

The trade name is the business-facing name used in operations and marketing. It may be more recognizable to customers, but it does not replace the corporate legal name in formal legal documents.

## Why companies use different names

The article gives several reasons:

- legal registration constraints differ from marketing needs
- similar trade names may sometimes be tolerated regionally even when corporate names are not
- a more memorable or attractive public-facing brand may be preferred
- legal rights and obligations still attach to the corporate name

## Why this matters in practice

The article stresses that many people identify a company by its trade name, not by the registered corporate name. That creates room for confusion in real transactions.

It warns that if invoices, tax documents, contracts, or marketing materials use a name that does not clearly connect to the underlying legal entity, disputes may arise over who the actual contracting party was.

## Corporate registry view

The article explains that the corporate registry prominently shows the registered legal company name, along with items such as:

- representative name
- registered office
- business purpose

By contrast, the trade name is not necessarily displayed as a separate formal field in the same way.

## Practical precautions

The article recommends the following when a company uses a trade name different from its corporate name:

- secure trade-name registration or trademark protection
- use the corporate legal name clearly on invoices and tax documents
- include the legal entity name and business registration details in contracts
- show the corporate name on websites and promotional materials

## Naming considerations

When choosing a legal or business name, the article advises checking:

- duplicate or similar names in the relevant registry
- similarity within the same region and business category
- trademark conflict risk
- branding clarity, memorability, and future scalability

It also notes that legal-form markers such as “Corporation” or “Limited Company” must be handled properly in the legal company name.

## Bottom line

The article’s main message is simple: a corporate name and a trade name are not the same thing, and treating them as interchangeable can create avoidable legal and business risk.
