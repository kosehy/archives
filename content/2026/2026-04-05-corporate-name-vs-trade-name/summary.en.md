# Summary — Corporate Name vs. Trade Name

## Core idea

This article explains that a company’s registered legal name and its outward-facing trade name are different concepts, and confusing them can create legal and operational risk.

## Key points

- The corporate legal name is the officially registered name used in formal legal contexts.
- The trade name is the public-facing name used in business operations and marketing.
- A company may use different names legally and commercially.
- Contracts, invoices, and tax documents should clearly identify the legal entity.
- If a separate trade name is used, trademark or registration protection may be necessary.
- Name selection should consider registry conflicts, trademark issues, and branding clarity together.

## Bottom line

The article argues that understanding the distinction between corporate name and trade name is a basic but important step in preventing avoidable business disputes.
