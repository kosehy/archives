## One-paragraph summary

This report argues that Claude adoption is not only spreading across more tasks, but that the quality of use changes with experience. Anthropic finds that Claude.ai usage has become more diverse and somewhat less concentrated in high-value tasks, while API usage continues shifting toward more programmatic and work-transforming workflows. Most importantly, long-tenure users appear to use Claude more effectively: they bring more complex and work-related tasks, choose stronger models more selectively, and achieve higher measured success rates, suggesting that AI capability is partly unlocked through learning-by-doing.

## Key points

- Claude.ai use has diversified, with top tasks accounting for a smaller share of traffic than in late 2025.
- Lower-value and more casual tasks have become a somewhat larger share of Claude.ai usage as adoption broadens.
- API usage remains more associated with coding, automation, and workflows likely to affect work more directly.
- Users appear to match Opus to harder and higher-value tasks, especially in API environments.
- High-tenure users collaborate with Claude more, use it more for work, and succeed more often in conversations.
- These differences may reflect both actual learning and selection effects such as early-adopter sophistication.
- Adoption inequality persists globally even as usage converges somewhat across US states.
- The report suggests that learning curves in AI usage may reinforce existing labor-market inequality.

## Memorable takeaway

AI adoption is not just about access; the people who learn how to use it well may pull further ahead over time.
