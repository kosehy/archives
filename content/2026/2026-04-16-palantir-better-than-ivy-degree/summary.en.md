# Summary

This Fortune article describes Palantir CEO Alex Karp’s argument that working at Palantir is a more valuable credential than a degree from elite universities. The piece frames his comments within broader anxiety about Gen Z employment, student debt, and the declining prestige of degrees relative to skills.

The article emphasizes Palantir’s anti-credential rhetoric, its Meritocracy Fellowship for high school graduates, and its criticism of opaque university admissions. At the same time, it highlights a tension: Palantir says it wants exceptional young talent, yet Karp also says he aims to grow revenue while shrinking headcount through AI-driven efficiency.
