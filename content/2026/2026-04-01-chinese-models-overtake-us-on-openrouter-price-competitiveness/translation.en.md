# Chinese models surpass U.S. rivals on price competitiveness, dominate top OpenRouter rankings

Original: https://www.aitimes.com/news/articleView.html?idxno=207427
Saved: 2026-04-01
Translation note: Smoothed into natural English for readability while preserving the article’s claims and framing.

Chinese models including MiniMax have overtaken U.S. models in OpenRouter’s AI token usage rankings by combining low prices with strong coding performance.

MiniMax M2.45 ranked first on OpenRouter’s weekly token-usage chart with 1.73 trillion tokens and has held the top spot for three consecutive weeks since its release on February 13.

DeepSeek V.32 ranked third with 840 billion tokens, Moonshot AI’s Kimi K2.5 ranked fourth with 685 billion, and Zhipu AI’s GLM 5 ranked eighth with 585 billion.

Among U.S. models, Google’s Gemini 3 Flash placed second with 1.07 trillion tokens, while Anthropic’s Claude Sonnet 4.6 ranked fifth with 663 billion.

In other words, several Chinese models released before the Lunar New Year holiday all rose into the top tier. The South China Morning Post described this as MiniMax ending a full year of U.S. dominance.

From the 9th to the 15th of last month, Chinese models recorded 4.11 trillion total tokens, surpassing the U.S. total of 2.94 trillion for the first time in the platform’s history. From the 16th to the 22nd, Chinese model usage surged again to 5.16 trillion tokens, while U.S. models fell to 2.7 trillion. The trend continued through the final week of February.

The article attributes this mainly to China’s overwhelming price competitiveness. MiniMax M2.5 costs $0.295 per million input tokens and $1.2 per million output tokens. That is 16 to 20 times cheaper than Claude Opus 4.6, priced at $5 and $25.

The article argues that Chinese AI companies are now taking market share by offering models that may be slightly weaker than top American frontier models but are dramatically cheaper.

At the same time, it notes an important limitation: OpenRouter’s numbers do not represent total global model usage. The platform is used mainly by individual developers and small startups that frequently switch models to reduce costs.

As a result, cheaper models tend to dominate there regardless of whether they are American or Chinese. For example, Gemini 3 Flash, which ranked second, costs $0.5 and $3, while xAI’s Grok 4.1 Fast, which ranked sixth, costs $0.2 and $0.5, making its pricing comparable to Chinese models.

U.S. companies are also cutting prices aggressively. Even Anthropic, which has historically been among the most expensive, reportedly reduced API prices for its Claude 4.5 and 4.6 lineups to about one-third of previous levels.

Still, the article emphasizes that Chinese AI models are beginning to shake a U.S.-led AI market by pushing a strong value-for-money strategy.

It also notes that token consumption is exploding as Chinese models are used more actively in AI agents and automated coding workflows that go beyond simple question-answering.

According to a Frost & Sullivan report published in February, the average daily token usage of Chinese companies reached 37 trillion in the second half of 2025, up 263% from 10.2 trillion in the first half.
