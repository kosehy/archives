# Summary

This LinkedIn post argues that many of the people who went on to define modern AI gathered at Google not simply because Google had money or respected research, but because Google had the largest and most urgent product-scale problems. In that view, breakthroughs like Transformer and TPU emerged not from abstract curiosity alone, but from product bottlenecks that demanded solutions.

The post’s broader thesis is that the strongest organizational asset may not be technology itself, but the size and seriousness of the problem an organization is forced to solve. Big problems attract strong people, shape research direction, and eventually help create the people who go on to build the next industry.
