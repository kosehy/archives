## One-paragraph summary

The article argues that software companies can no longer survive in the old “comfortable middle” between growth and profitability. As AI reshapes product economics and customer spending, management teams need to choose one of two clear paths: either reignite growth through truly AI-native products that can materially move topline performance within 12–18 months, or radically rebuild the business to achieve 40%+ true operating margins including stock-based compensation. Half-measures, superficial layoffs, and incremental product add-ons are presented as inadequate for the new market reality.

## Key points

- Public markets have already repriced software companies as growth slows and valuation multiples compress.
- Many companies still look weaker than they appear once stock-based compensation is treated as a real expense.
- The article says the middle ground — neither fast-growing nor deeply profitable — will become increasingly unattractive.
- Path 1 is to build net-new AI-native products that can add 10+ points of revenue growth.
- That path requires a fast internal rebuild: identifying high-leverage talent, reshaping leadership, and organizing R&D into small, fast-moving teams.
- It also requires new business models centered more on tokens, usage, automations, and machine-driven workflows rather than traditional seat-based pricing.
- Path 2 is to rebuild the company around true profitability, targeting 40–50%+ operating margins including SBC.
- That means much deeper structural change than modest layoffs: flattening management, simplifying products and services, tightening pricing, and rethinking what the company’s moat really is.
- Even on the profitability path, AI should still change how engineering teams work, including heavier use of tokens and much higher output per engineer.
- The author’s bottom line is that founders and boards must choose a path quickly instead of drifting in between.

## Memorable takeaway

Grow by a meaningful new AI product wave, or rebuild into a cash-generating machine. The middle lane is disappearing.
