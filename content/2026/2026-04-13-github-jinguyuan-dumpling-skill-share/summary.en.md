## One-paragraph summary

This LinkedIn post highlights a Chinese dumpling restaurant that published an AI skill interface, framing it as a sign that small local businesses are beginning to design products and services for AI agents as well as humans. The author connects this example to broader Chinese AI adoption, public support for agent businesses, and the rapid spread of AI assistants like Doubao and Yuanbao. The post’s broader claim is that once AI becomes a practical customer interface, product design itself starts to change, with conventional human-facing controls giving way to agent-driven workflows.

## Key points

- A long-running dumpling restaurant in Beijing reportedly exposed its services through an AI skill.
- The post connects this to large-scale AI assistant adoption in China.
- It argues that local businesses are starting to build interfaces for AI, not just people.
- The author links this trend to similar changes in enterprise products already being operated through coding agents.

## Memorable takeaway

The deeper shift is not that businesses are adopting AI tools, but that they may start designing their interfaces for AI as a customer.
