# Summary

This Hacker News discussion shows that some people do use OpenClaw in practice, especially for personal assistant tasks, memory management, family archiving, and low-friction household automation. But it also shows why adoption remains contentious: costs can be high, permissions feel risky, and many technically skilled users believe scripts or narrower tools would work better.

The thread’s deeper split seems to be that OpenClaw feels empowering to users who want conversational automation, while experienced programmers often judge it against deterministic tooling they could build themselves.
