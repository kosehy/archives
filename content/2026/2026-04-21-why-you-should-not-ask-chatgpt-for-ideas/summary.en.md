# Summary

This article argues that chatbot-style creative tools can reduce originality by locking users into the first result they see, a phenomenon the cited researchers call design fixation. It presents HAICo as a better alternative because it forces idea exploration before output generation, separating divergent thinking from convergent refinement.

The broader practical lesson is that AI users should ask for multiple conceptual directions first, and only then refine a selected one, rather than immediately polishing the first generated artifact.
