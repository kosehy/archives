# Summary — Harness hype vs. measured reality

## Core idea

This post argues that harnesses can produce real gains, but those gains should be measured carefully and should not be confused with genuine increases in model intelligence.

## Key points

- The author reviews three papers rather than relying on intuition or hype.
- Meta-Harness is presented as a case where AI improves the harness itself with better accuracy and lower token cost.
- Natural-Language Agent Harnesses improves task success, but with very large cost increases.
- ARC-AGI-3 is used to argue that handcrafted harnesses can inflate benchmark performance without demonstrating general intelligence.
- The post draws a line between automation tools and actual AGI-like reasoning.
- The long-term challenge is adaptation and action efficiency in unfamiliar environments.

## One-line takeaway

Harnesses can make systems work much better, but better harnesses do not automatically mean smarter models.
