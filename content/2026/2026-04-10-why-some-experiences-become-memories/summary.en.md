# Summary — Why do some experiences become memories while others fade away?

## Core idea

This article explains memory as a selective editing process rather than simple recording. According to the piece, the balance between pyramidal cells and inhibitory neurons determines which experiences are stabilized into long-term memory and which are discarded.

## Key points

- Pyramidal cells encode and transmit incoming information in the hippocampus and cortex.
- Left on their own, they would react to too much information and overwhelm the brain with noise.
- Inhibitory neurons regulate this activity by suppressing irrelevant signals and sharpening meaningful ones.
- Stable memory depends on the rhythmic interaction between these two cell types.
- Disruption in this balance may be linked to learning difficulty, schizophrenia, and anxiety disorders.
- Rest and sleep matter because the brain uses downtime to filter noise and consolidate important signals.

## One-line takeaway

The article argues that memory is not just about storing information well, but about actively deleting most of what the brain receives.
