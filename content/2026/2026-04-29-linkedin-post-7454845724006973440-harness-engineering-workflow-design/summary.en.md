# Summary (en)

This LinkedIn post reframes “working well” in the AI era through prompting and harness engineering. The author argues that writing good prompts is useful, but the higher-level capability is building an environment where good prompts and verification loops emerge repeatedly.

The key distinction is between:

1. **A good prompter** — someone who can decide what to ask, how to ask it, and how to verify the result within one turn.
2. **A harness builder** — someone who designs memory, skills, triggers, and workflows so good inputs and validations are generated by the system itself.

The post also proposes a better interview question than “How well do you use AI?”: “When was the last time you rebuilt your AI workflow? What did you throw away, and why did you decide it no longer worked?” These questions reveal whether a person has ever treated their own workflow as an object to inspect and improve.

Finally, the author argues that Korean education is not merely memorization-oriented; it is optimized for executing fixed procedures quickly inside a given problem space. That execution skill is valuable, but it leaves underdeveloped the ability to design, discard, and rebuild procedures.
