## One-paragraph summary
This repository packages production-oriented engineering practices as reusable “skills” for AI coding agents, aiming to make agents follow disciplined workflows across the full development lifecycle. It maps slash commands such as `/spec`, `/plan`, `/build`, `/test`, `/review`, and `/ship` to structured processes, and backs them with twenty lifecycle skills, specialist review personas, and supporting reference checklists. The core idea is that agents need explicit workflow scaffolding, verification gates, and anti-rationalization mechanisms if they are to produce work closer to senior-engineer standards rather than prototype-grade output.

## Key points
- The repo turns software engineering practices into structured skills for AI agents.
- It covers the whole lifecycle from ideation and planning to testing, review, and shipping.
- It supports multiple agent environments including Claude Code, Cursor, Gemini CLI, and others.
- It emphasizes process, verification, and anti-shortcut discipline over generic prompting.

## Memorable takeaway
The point is not to give agents more advice, but to give them enforceable workflows.
